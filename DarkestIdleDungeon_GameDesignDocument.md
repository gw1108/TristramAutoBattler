Game is called Darkest Idle Dungeon
Genre is idle, rpg, town builder, incremental game.
Art
The horizon shows a decrepit and ruined town, but it slowly gets rebuilt as the player purchases upgrades.
The general style is 2D pixel art. The characters do not animate as they walk but instead rotate slightly back and forth on the bottom center while squash and stretching. Most animations should be efficient 2D tweens driven through code rather than keyframes or sprites.

Gameplay Overview - 4 main sections of gameplay

The loop: you start the day with random adventurers at the inn for hiring. You hire them and they move into the city square. The player can optionally purchase upgrades to buildings. Everything costs gold which can be gained from diving into the dungeon after they defeat an enemy party. Then they go to the dungeon, which has a bell next to its entrance; ringing the bell starts the call to arms. (What the dungeon actually looks like — a demon infested church, a mine, etc. — is up to a future visualization task.) If the player calls to arms while there are no adventurers in the town (for example because they ran out of money and could not hire anyone), 1-2 random level 1 adventurers will always show up in the city square for free, so the player can never get permanently stuck. You can move through a town with a top-down position with WASD in the pre-dungeon raid gameplay. You will travel to different shop vendors to hire adventurers, build item shops, build training shops. Each shop has a different upgrade tree.

Interacting with things: nearly every interactable supports two input methods. You can left click the interactable and your character will automatically pathfind toward it and open the relevant UI once close enough, OR you can manually approach it and press the interact button. For example, to show the upgrade UI for a building you can left click the appropriate part of the building and your character will pathfind there and open the UI, or you can walk up to that same part of the building and press interact. Likewise, to view an adventurer's stats you can click them and your character will approach and pull up their UI once close enough, or you can approach them and press interact. There is no HUD in the town view; the only overlaid UI is the proximity prompt that appears when the player is near an interactable.

You can left click heroes to view their stats in a panel. The hero's name is the panel title, with their epithet as a subtitle below it (e.g. title "Aldric", subtitle "The Unyielding"). Both randomly generated. At the top left of the panel, hanging fully outside the panel's edge at the same vertical position as the title, is a circle containing the hero's class icon, with the hero's level shown in a shield banner directly below the circle. At the top right, also outside the panel's edge, is a matching circle containing a gold bag icon, with a number below it indicating the adventurer's personal wealth (gold is shown here and is not listed among the stats). A line separator then their stats which consist of a stat icon, a “:”, then the stat as a number or percent. The first stat row is HP, Power, Morale. The second row is HP Regen, Morale Regen. All remaining stats follow after. Then a separator labeled "Inventory" followed by the hero's items where each item is shown as an icon + item name label. Then a separator and an activity section describing what the adventurer is currently doing: hireable adventurers show "Looking for work", hired adventurers show their current town activity (e.g. "Waiting around", "Chatting with Aldric"). For hireable adventurers there is then another separator followed by their class name and class description, both center aligned, and below that 2 buttons labeled "Hire (X Gold)" and "Hire and Sponsor (X Gold)" where X is the cost. Hire and Sponsor is the same as hire, but directly gives the adventurer money so they can buy better equipment from your own shops immediately.

Hired adventurers wander around the town and do things that make sense for themselves depending on how much money they have. If they can afford their next equipment upgrade they go buy it from the appropriate shop. If they don't have enough money for the next upgrade they instead do something flavorful like waiting around, eating, chatting with other adventurers, working out, bathing, training, or playing with the dog. Their current activity is what shows in the activity section of their stat panel.

Once you call to arms the adventurers randomly form up to 3 parties of 1-3 adventurers each. Clicking a hero during this phase opens their normal hero stat panel, but with one extra section at the bottom that fits on a single horizontal row: a "Party:" label, a Kick button, a Swap button, and an "X/Y Actions Today" label. Y is the number of party actions the player can take per day, determined by the player's stats (3 by default); X counts down as actions are spent. Swap button will randomly swap that adventurer with another adventurer in a different party. Kick will swap that adventurer with an adventurer that is not in a party. If there are not enough adventurers available, the kick button does nothing and the kick button is disabled. This can happen if the total number of adventurers in the town is less than or equal to 9. Once the player is satisfied with the parties formed they can move to the dungeon and hit enter to trigger the next stage of the game. 

In the dungeon dive the screen is equally split into 3 separate rows for each party. Each row shows its party's 1-3 character portraits in the top left of the row. There is no other overlay UI during the dive: no battle counter, no boss indicator, and no gold, dungeon level, or day display. Each party moves left to right through its row, exposing the fog of war as it advances. Enemies — except for the boss at the end of the level — show up and intercept the adventurers as they progress; the turn-based auto battle is fought then and there, and after winning the party moves forward again, repeating this until they finally reach the boss. Defeating the boss exposes the stairs down to the next dungeon level, or, if this is the final level of the dungeon, the party celebrates on the spot because the game has been won. A party keeps going until they flee from morale loss, they all die, or they complete the level. If a row's party wipes or flees, that row continues to exist for the rest of the dive but nothing further happens in it visually. The dungeon has 3 levels. Each dungeon level consists of 4-5 battles of increasing difficulty, culminating in a boss battle as the final battle. Only 1 party needs to defeat the boss, at any point, for that level to be completed; completing a level unlocks the next dungeon level for all future expeditions (dungeon level 1 is always unlocked). Each party tackles the hardest unlocked dungeon level they think they can accomplish, judged by their character levels (e.g. the party's average level) against a rough range per dungeon level: dungeon level 1 fits any character level, dungeon level 2 fits characters around level 4 ± 2 (about 2-6), dungeon level 3 around level 6 ± 3 (about 3-9), and in general dungeon level N fits around level 2N ± N, with the ± spread capped at about 3 (so dungeon level 4 fits around level 8 ± 3). Parties pick the highest unlocked level whose range they fit, so different parties can traverse different dungeon levels in the same expedition. The player is expected to beat the 1st level in 3 days, 2nd level in 5 days, and 3rd level in 9 days assuming they upgrade as much as possible between days in an optimal way. These day targets are per level, not cumulative. Each dungeon level has its own biome: level 1 is a grassy field with some trees, like a gentle forest; level 2 is a swamp, so it looks like a swampy forest; level 3's biome is TBD. When the player conquers the 3rd and final dungeon level they have won the game. Winning unlocks endless mode, where they can continue to challenge the dungeon endlessly.

Combat Mechanics
Each character has a speed trait that determines what order they go in. Then they will generally use their ability to attack, heal, aoe attack, or defensively attack. Each attack has a damage + morale damage. If a character’s health hits 0 or morale hits 0 they will die or flee. Adventurers that die will get added to the graveyard unless the graveyard is full in which case they will overwrite the oldest grave. A fleeing character safely goes back to the town square.

Class roles (gameplay). Adventurers generally have a single target attack unless noted otherwise.
Knight: more defensive abilities and tanky.
Captain: morale healing and boosts his comrades' damage. Ok health, tankiness, and damage.
Berserker: high damage. Ok health and tankiness.
Mage: med-high AOE attack. Low defense and low health.
Rogue: high single target attack. Ok health and ok defense.
Cleric: does not have an attack; focuses on healing and defensive buffs. Ok health and ok defense.

Adventurers have different stats. Health, max morale, morale regen, power which determines the damage, gold, hp regen, guard % which determines the chance to block an attack (max 90%), armor which reduces damage by the amount of armor the adventurer has, evasion which is pitted against the attack accuracy stat of the enemy, crit chance which on attack gives a chance to deal crit damage bonus, crit damage bonus which defaults to 50% meaning the crit attack deals 1.5x the damage, Accuracy which determines if the attack lands depending on the enemy’s evasion. The formula below is used to calculate the chance to Hit, where AA is the total Accuracy Rating of the attacker and DE is the total Evasion Rating of the defender.
Chance to Hit = AA * 1.25 * 100 / ( AA + DE * 0.3 )
The formula's result can exceed 100%, but any value above 100% does nothing extra — the chance to Hit is capped at 100%. The chance to Hit is also floored at 5%, meaning the chance to Evade a Hit can never exceed 95%.
Evasion and Accuracy are raw ratings, not percents. When displaying either stat as a percent in the UI, substitute the missing side of the formula (AA or DE) with the average value of an enemy of equal level. For example, an adventurer's displayed evasion percent uses the average accuracy of enemies at their level as AA and their own evasion rating as DE.

Economy
The player starts with 19 gold. Rebuilding a ruined building costs 10 gold. Hiring a fresh level 1 adventurer costs 7-9 gold, and each adventurer level beyond 1 increases the hiring cost by 4-7 gold. The hire + sponsor option doubles the hiring cost.

Balance
Specific stat numbers should be determined through playtesting; a first draft is written up in DarkestIdleDungeon_BalanceNumbers.md. Two anchor points to tune against: a level 1 Mage should deal 1-3 AOE damage as a med-low to med-high damaging character, and a Captain should be able to take 6-7 attacks (by dodging some of them) from the very first level 1 enemy encounter before dying.

There is a post dungeon expedition summary panel which shows the party adventuring results. The title is “Expedition Summary Day X” where “X” is replaced with the current day. The day is just a count of how many expeditions have been made. So the first expedition is day 1. Second is day 2. Etc. The panel is sized to about 80% of the window both vertically and horizontally, and the results area has a vertical scroll bar in case the content doesn't fit on screen. There is a column for each party that went out; all columns are the same width, and each column always reserves enough vertical space for 3 adventurers. At the top of each column, above the party results, is a visual of the dungeon level that party traversed (see dungeon level biomes). One by one left to right each column animates the results. The xp bar fills up for each party member according to how much xp they earned. To the left of the xp bar is the member's current level, and to the right of the bar is the next level, both shown as a number on a shield banner in the same style as the hero stat panel's level indicator. If they level up it visually showcases that they advanced to the next level and their level increases. To the right of the xp bar (after the next-level banner) their earned gold is shown as a pile of coins that looks progressively more full, luxurious, and rich the more gold was earned: 1 visual coin per 3-6 gold earned (minimum 1 coin if any gold was earned), maxing out at 100 visual coins. If a member earned 0 gold no gold is shown at all. Dead adventurers cannot earn gold, so they never show gold, and they do not show an xp bar either. If that adventurer dies we animate that they died by rotating their character sprite to be lying down and tint it red + blood splatter. There is also a status text that says “Fled”, “Heroically cleared the way to X” where X is the next level, “Conquered the final dungeon”, or “Murdered by a X” where X is the enemy that killed them (it can also replace murder with Slain/Killed/Defeated/Crushed/Devastated/Demolished/Destroyed” below their name and appears to denote the final result of that character. Finally a close button to close the panel.

Inn
This shop has a section where adventurers can be hired. Left click an adventurer to open the hiring UI (your character will pathfind to them and it opens once close enough), or approach them and press the interact button. It will display their name + cost + class + level.

The inn also has a door like every other shop where you can upgrade the inn itself. The inn starts the game at level 1 and does not need to be rebuilt. You can upgrade the inn level itself which unlocks additional upgrades, upgrade the hero capacity so each day you have more heroes, improves the chance of an elite version of a hero for recruitment, reduces the cost of recruitment.

The hero item shop allows heroes of the appropriate level to upgrade their equipment. Upgrades like unlock level 0-5 (5 being max level) equipment, improve defensive ability of level 0-5 equipment, improve offensive ability of level 0-5 equipment.

The training shop is flavored as the training grounds. Here you can upgrade the training shop itself, improve hero experience gain rate, improve hero base offensive stats from level 0-5, improve hero base defensive stats from level 0-5 give heroes improved experience gain for 1-3 dungeons after they get hired, give heroes base xp when they get hired, and increase the amount of gold you get from the dungeon.
