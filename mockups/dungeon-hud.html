Dungeon Dive — 3-Row Auto Battle with Fog of War

Screen split into equal rows, one per non-empty party (up to 3) — an empty party gets no row. The party's 1-3 character portraits sit in the top left of their row. The only overlay is the Horn of Retreat button in the top right, and only once it has been bought from the inn's upgrade tree — pressing it makes every party flee at once, ending the expedition. It is the player's sole input during a dive. Otherwise no overlays: no battle counter, boss indicator, gold, dungeon level, or day. Parties march left to right, exposing the fog of war (dark right side); enemies — except the boss — intercept them mid-row and the battle is fought in place; ✕ marks defeated encounters left behind. Beating the boss reveals the stairs down, or on the final level the party celebrates (game won). Red bar = HP, blue = morale, yellow ▼ = whose turn. The three rows below show three states: mid-progress interception, boss fight, and post-boss.

Pacing: every character's turn takes a fixed 0.6s whatever they do; walking takes 4s on entry and between fights. That puts level 1 battle 1 around 6-9s, a genuinely even low-DPS fight around 25s, the dungeon 4 boss around 18-36s, and a full level 1 clear around 65s. All three rows run concurrently in real time, so a dive lasts as long as its slowest row, not the sum. There is deliberately no speed control, because the Horn of Retreat stays the player's only input during a dive.
